# Economics Search Guidance

Use this file when choosing databases, prioritizing papers, or explaining why certain papers are screened in or out.

## Database roles

- `EconLit`: best for targeted economics searches and JEL-oriented discovery
- `NBER`: best for recent working papers and active research fronts
- `Google Scholar`: best for broad discovery and citation tracing
- `RePEc/IDEAS`: best for economics-specific discovery, author pages, working-paper links, and citation trails
- `JSTOR`: best for established journal articles and archival coverage

## Quality heuristics

Prefer papers that are:
- Recent, unless an older paper is clearly seminal
- Published in respected economics journals or major field journals
- Written by authors with strong records in the topic
- Associated with highly ranked economics departments, policy schools, central banks, or top research institutes
- Methodologically credible and transparent

Treat these as heuristics for prioritization, not as substitutes for evaluating the actual paper.

## Review lens for economics papers

Focus on:
- Key research question
- Main research method
- Main findings
- Contribution to the literature

Also capture:
- Data and setting
- Identification strategy
- Important caveats
- How the paper fits with nearby studies
